My Story

Steve Chen, a student of life and knowledge sponge, was born in 1992 to two first generation immigrants starting a new life in Maryland. Oldest of 5, Steve has struggled and succeeded in many of his endeavors and supports his siblings through example.

During his younger childhood years he was extremely shy and unable to effectively communicate with others. His passions began leaning toward competitive videogames, sometimes clocking in at 30+ hours a week during summers. There in the comfort of his own introverted state he broke out of his shell, became a leader, build a large organic following on Youtube and TwitchTV, and make lifelong friends around the world. He lead 2-9 person eSport teams across the US and came back with a sense of pride and a following from all accross the US and Asia. His passion for competitive gaming eventually lead to a big decline to his health. Over the course of his teenage years he became Class II obese due his sedentary nature and poor eating habits stemming from a child hood of fast food and lack of guidance.

At the age of 19, he knew he had to change. He was intrigued by the low-carb movement and started dieting. He failed 7 times over the course of 2 years and gained all the weight back. At his largest he was almost 240 pounds at 5’8. But everytime he failed he learned something new, how to become better, what foods to eat, and his own weaknesses. Currently he’s lost over 40lbs and lives a productive lifestyle. From this he began to explore new hobbies and transitioned from a basement dwelling gamer to an Web Developing outdoorsman. Extremely curious about the local Maryland wilderness and man-made lakes. He’s self-taught himself fly fishing and kayaking.

His motto in life is "Try everything once". His outlook: time is limited and to break out of an introverted shell you need to be able to adapt. Bad or good, as long as you're alive and free there’s no such thing to lose. By trying new things at least once he’ll be moving forward into a better now, if not… at least you got tomorrow to try something else, or try once more.
